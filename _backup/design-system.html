Visual Identity Specification

Editorial × Engineering × Institutional

A design system for work in agentic AI, cross-border payments, research, and public policy. Every page should read like a research paper, a standards document, or a central bank report — not a marketing site. Quiet confidence. Precision. Longevity.

§ 2

Color System

Paper
#F7F7F5
Background. Warm paper white. Feels like a research paper, not a bright webpage.
Graphite
#3B3F45
Primary text and UI. Headings, body, icons, borders, navigation.
Sky
#C7D8E8
Accent, used sparingly. Links, highlights, hover states, charts.

Usage Ratio

PAPER 85–90% · GRAPHITE 8–12% · SKY 1–3%

§ 3

Dark Mode

Invert the system. Background becomes Graphite #3B3F45. Text becomes Paper #F7F7F5. The Sky accent stays the same. Spacing, typography, and layout do not change.

Try it Use the Dark Mode button in the header of this page. Only the two base colors swap. Nothing else moves.
§ 4

Typography

Serif · Long-form reading IBM Plex Serif / Source Serif
The infrastructure of trust is built slowly, verified constantly, and noticed only when it fails.
Sans-serif · UI IBM Plex Sans / Inter
Interface text stays plain and direct. Navigation, buttons, labels, and body copy use one or two weights at most.
Monospace · Data & metadata IBM Plex Mono / JetBrains Mono

Principles

  • WeightsMinimal. Two or three per family. Hierarchy comes from size and space, not boldness.
  • WhitespaceGenerous. When in doubt, add space.
  • SpacingConsistent scale across all surfaces.
  • HierarchyEditorial hierarchy over decoration.
